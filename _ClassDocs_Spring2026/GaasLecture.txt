Separate definitions for Live Service Games and GaaS?
Live-service game: A game that requires an always-online connection or provides continuous online content/updates and community-driven events (examples: World of Warcraft, Destiny).
Games as a Service (GaaS): A commercial model framing a game as an ongoing service rather than a one-time product; emphasizes recurring monetization, content updates, and a license-based access rather than an owned product (examples: League of Legends, Fortnite, Overwatch).
